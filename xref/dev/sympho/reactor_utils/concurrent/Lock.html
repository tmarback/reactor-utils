5   /**
6    * A simple lock.
7    * 
8    * <p>Unlike a normal lock, the locks provided by this type are not bound to a particular
9    * thread, and so any thread with a reference to the returned lock may release it.
10   * 
11   * <p>Be careful that this implies that the lock is <b>NOT reentrant</b>, attempting to
12   * acquire a lock twice will always fail.
13   *
14   * @version 1.0
15   * @since 1.0
16   * @apiNote While binding the ownership of a lock to an instance instead of a thread
17   *          arguably makes it harder to achieve concurrency safety (due to it still
18   *          being possible to make concurrent accesses after acquiring the lock, as
19   *          it is not bound to a particular thread), it is necessary in the context
20   *          of reactive streams where a single chain of operations may execute in
21   *          different threads.
22   */
23  public interface Lock {
24      
25      /**
26       * Acquires the lock, if currently available.
27       *
28       * @return The acquired lock, or {@code null} if the lock is in use and could not be acquired.
29       */
30      @Nullable AcquiredLock tryAcquire();
31  
32  }
